About Mateete Comprehensive Seed Secondary School

From humble beginnings, Mateete Comprehensive Seed Secondary School took its first breath in 2003, then known simply as Mateete Secondary School. Under the gentle but determined guidance of the late Madam Namatovu Evelyne, its pioneer headteacher, the school opened its doors to just thirty hopeful learners — thirty seeds planted in the soil of a new dream.
Those early days were not without storms. The school faced trials that tested its roots — scarcity of resources, limited classrooms, and the constant struggle to build from almost nothing. Yet, like a sapling reaching for sunlight, Mateete grew stronger with each challenge, nourished by the dedication of teachers and the resilience of its students.
Today, that small seed has blossomed into a thriving institution under the stewardship of Mr. Ssendagire Hamuza, its current headteacher. The journey from thirty students to a vibrant community stands as a living testament to perseverance, vision, and the unyielding spirit of growth.

By Kaweesi G. William

Biology Teacher who witnessed the founding of the school

Standby Generator

                  
Reliable electricity is essential for the smooth running of academic and administrative activities at Mateete Comprehensive Seed Secondary School. However, frequent power outages have often disrupted lessons, computer studies, examinations, and office operations. In order to ensure continuity of learning and administrative efficiency, the school proposes the purchase of a standby generator.

                    This project aims to provide a stable and reliable source of power that will support classrooms, laboratories, the computer room, and staff offices during times of power interruption. The generator will also enhance security by ensuring outdoor and corridor lighting remains functional at night.
